Tasty Meal Plan

This project was done for a senior level interface design class. The project brief was to study an existing application by looking into its primary goal, key features, interface design and interaction patterns. Through conducting user research and interviews, the goal of the project is to design and prototype a new feature.

Project Description

Duration May - June 2020 (3 weeks)
Team Amy Liao, Bella Wang
Roles Interface Design, User Research, Wireframing, Prototyping
Tools Figma
Contribution I conducted primary user research and interviews, developed user personas, copywrote content for the proposal pitch, and worked on the design of the final feature interface.

Research Phase

Understanding the Tasty App

In order to design for Tasty, we first had to familiarize ourselves with the app and the company itself. Tasty is brand under BuzzFeed that produces recipe videos and trendy food series that are shared across various social media. The app serves as a platform that gathers their recipe videos for users to easily access. The primary purpose of the application is to teach users how to cook easily with visuals and written instructions, along with a detailed ingredient list.

User Research

We conducted an online survey to gather general data surrounding cooking and eating habits for potential users. None of our survey participants had used the Tasty app before.

Details of our survey results, including demongraphics and behaviours surrounding cooking at home.

From there, we had further video interviews with 6 participants to ask detailed questions regarding their cooking habits. We then observed how the participants used the app as a first time user and asked them to perform various tasks while screen-sharing through a Zoom call. The findings were as follows:

01

Users were frustrated that they had to keep filtering for recipes that fit their dietary restrictions.

02

Users were unable to customize their list of saved recipes.

03

Users had to keep a mental note on any adjustments on recipes they have tried before.

User Persona

Taking the insights we collected from our research, we created a persona to better represent our potential users. The persona gave us a model that helped narrow down our focus to possible areas we could design for.

Our persona for a potential user of the Tasty app including their goals, motivations, habits, and pain points.

Ideation Phase

Initial Framing

How might we improve the user’s experience by personalizing and catering to their needs?

Our design focus was framed around the goals, motivations, and frustrations of our potential user. During this ideation phase, we entered a brainstorming session to generate various feature ideas targeting areas surrounding their diet and lifestyle. Of those ideas, we selected three to further develop and created a mini-persona for each.

Three proposed features for our potential user.

Refining a Feature Proposal

Our team decided to move forward with the meal planning feature because we felt that it aligned most with our potential user’s needs and pain points. It would directly address their strict diet and cooking habits. Moreover, the feature would be able to utilize the Tasty app’s existing recipe nutritional information. Our initial user testing participants reported that it was interesting to have that information but saw no further use for it within the app. So we looked into expanding that function.We further developed our mini-persona from our previous proposal to include her experience and end goals with the Tasty app.

A mini persona with experience goals more specifically directed to the proposed meal plan feature.

Design Solution

Feature Goal

Tasty Meal Plan helps the user lead a healthier lifestyle and be organized with their diet through tracking the nutritional value of their planned meals throughout the week.

By incorporating a Meal Plan feature within Tasty’s app, we are able to take advantage of the nutritional data and turn it into a full-fledged feature, with the supporting functions of nutritional logging, nutritional information, and the weekly planner. We intend this feature will help users like Hailey lead a healthier lifestyle since she can easily see her progress by tracking her meals, specifically her calories. She is also able to substitute different ingredients and adjust to desirable servings in the recipes based on her preferences.

The three main components of our feature.

Feature Details

Nutrition Logging

This function allows the user to input their daily calories and nutritional information by scanning a barcode with their camera or to type it in manually with the keyboard. The user can also log their calories and nutritional information by searching for a specific recipe or by selecting the recommended recipes.

video

Nutrition Information

The user can access a detailed report summarizing their daily, weekly, monthly, and yearly dietary intake as well as view their progress towards their goal over time.

video

Weekly Planner

The Weekly Planner function aids the user in organizing their meals, where they can directly search for a Tasty recipe. After the user has created their meal plan, they can easily choose which recipes to log by selecting them.

video

Tone of Voice and Function Integration

Our feature introduces confirmation pop-ups that use encouraging language to foster a sense of accomplishment to the users. The functions present in the meal planning page are integrated into other areas of the app and can be accessed in any recipe item. I had suggested these ideas as important details to our feature as it would create an enjoyable and integrated experience for our users.

I had implemented the meal planning feature to existing app interfaces as well as created a cohesive tone of voice to assist the meal planning process.

Conclusion

Final Thoughts

Overall, this was a project that I enjoyed working on with my team. Due to the short timeframe, there were certain details that we were not able to fully refine. However, it allowed us to work efficiently through careful planning and keeping on track with each other. This was also my first time having to do user testing remotely, which was surprisingly not a huge hindrance to the project. We were still able to gain insights on the users' frustrations while using the Tasty app and observe how they navigated through the various tasks through screensharing and video calling tools.
