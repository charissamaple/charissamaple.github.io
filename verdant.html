Verdant

Verdant is an academic case study for an application created in a senior level user interface and user experience course at Simon Fraser University. For this project, we were placed with the task of establishing a domain to research and work within to propose a meaningful design intervention.

Project Description

Duration June - August 2020 (3 months)
Team Amy Liao, Bella Wang, Newton Nguyen-Le, Patrick Pacunana
Roles Interface Design, User Research, Visual Design, Wireframing, Prototyping
Tools Figma, Adobe Illustrator
Contribution I conducted primary research through user testing and interviews, as well as secondary research in the domain of study. Furthermore, I worked on sketches, wireframes, and high fidelity designs of the interface until finally prototyping the interactions.

Research Phase

User Research

Domain of Study - Environmental Awareness

Our team decided to explore the domain of environmental awareness due to the growing issue of global warming and carbon dioxide emissions. In order to better understand the domain and potential areas for intervention, we conducted a survey. This would help us to gain a sense of the current relationship individuals share with the environment and how they perceive their environmental impact. Our primary research allowed us to gather diverse responses from over 50 participants with ages ranging from 18-61 years old.

Domain of study and audience insights.

Key Insights

01

Carbon footprint is more than transportation emissions

When thinking of carbon footprint, individuals tend to associate their transportation methods with how positive or negative their impact is. However, there are other factors, such as purchases, that must be taken into consideration when calculating one’s overall carbon dioxide emission.

02

Eager to change, but unaware of how to

A common theme amongst the respondes was their willingness to change their lifestyle and take action to prevent further damage to the environment. However, they were unsure of exactly how their life currently impacts the Earth and what measures they should take to live more environmentally friendly.

Framing our Design Approach

Taking insights gained from our research, we narrowed our scope to focus on personal carbon dioxide emissions. With this clear direction, we established an end goal of ensuring that the design would be able to inform users of their carbon dioxide emissions in efforts to help reduce their personal carbon footprint. Design Process

Initial Proposal

After a rapid brainstorming session, our group decided to move forward with a carbon footprint tracking application. From the research and insights collected, we gathered data to create a persona that would better represent our potential users. In order to envision how our persona would interact with this type of system, we formulated a journey map to see potential touchpoints that we could use to drive the design of our application.

User persona with their short background, goals, and motivations.

User experience map for our persona's journey with our proposed area of intervention.

Design Iterations

After formulating the persona’s journey map, the team split up to iterate several versions of the application through wireframing and mapping out wireflows. I worked heavily on the profile page and data visualization of the user’s carbon emissions. With each iteration, the main carbon footprint graph as well as the display of the emission breakdown was experimented with to explore what would be most appropriate for the type of data given to the user.

Some of the screens from the home page that I had worked on throughout each iteration.

User Testing

Before being able to finalize the design, we conducted user testing to gather insights and feedback on the core workflows of our application. This was done by video interview (Zoom) with 14 participants using screen sharing to observe how they explored the application as a first time user to monitor how they naturally reacted with the design. Additionally, users were given key tasks to complete within the application. The user-testing allowed us to discover any concerns or thoughts that users had with our designs.

01

Lack of context

Users were given a large amount of data and unlabeled numbers that they were unable to understand. They were also confused whether the values should be interpreted as positive or negative since they were given no basis of comparison or recommended value.

02

Too many features

Our initial design did not feel as cohesive due us introducing many possible user flows without one clear end goal. For this reason, the core feature of tracking user’s carbon dioxide emissions felt incomplete. We would need to reduce clutters of information to put more focus on improving the experience of logging emission activities.

03

Not fully inclusive options

For logging transportation emissions, there were not enough options provided in regards to how people travel, whether it be through carpooling, driving more than one vehicle, or using a motorcycle as opposed to using a regular vehicle.

Design Solution

Mission Overview

Verdant’s purpose is to inform users of their carbon dioxide emissions in efforts to help reduce their personal carbon footprint. By providing a resource for users to track their carbon footprint, Verdant seeks to help individuals live a more eco-friendly lifestyle.

Experience Verdant here

Feature Details

Profile and Emission Breakdown

The profile page presents the user’s emissions that they have tracked over time. They are able to view detailed breakdowns of each category and recommendations based on their recent activity. Friends’ emissions can also be viewed below.

Logging Carbon Emission Activities

Through scanning their utility bills or manually inputting details, Verdant is able to track a user’s carbon dioxide emissions for both water and electricity usage as well as provide weekly averages. The transportation activity tracking allows users to split their emissions if they carpool with their friends.

Personal Goals

Users are encouraged to track their progress for their journey in becoming more sustainable with the use of the personal activity goals. The primary objective for Goals is to allow individuals to find new elements in their lifestyle that they can alter to reduce their emissions.

Conclusion

Final Thoughts

For Verdant, I was excited that we chose to work in the domain of environmental awareness, as it was an area that I had previously been interested in but had not done a project directed at the issue before. My team and I initially struggled with understanding the details of how carbon footprint was calculated and in turn did a lot of secondary research related to the matter. I enjoyed being able to see our progress over each design iteration alongside the feedback received from user testing. I was overall satisfied with the final product that the team was able to complete!
